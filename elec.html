Intel NUC

AcYut houses an on-board Intel NUC (Next Unit of Computing) D34010WYKH with Ubuntu 14.04.02 LTS installed.

Inertial Measurement Unit

AcYut uses an 6 Degree-Of-Freedom Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) manufactured by Xsens. It can be used to measure the bot's acceleration and orientation, using an internal combination of accelerometers and gyroscopes. The IMU is used to localize the robot using yaw angle to determine the present heading, and to stabilize it by detecting disturbances using the roll and pitch angles.

Camera

An IDS uEye LE Industrial Camera is attached to the 'head' of the robot to give it the power to perceive its environment and respond accordingly. A fish-eye lens is used to provide a wider field of view.

Motors

The robot has 23 Dynamixel Servo Motors, out of which 12 (in the legs) are Dynamixel EX-106 motors and the other 11 are Dynamixel RX-64 motors. A USB to RS485 board and a TTL level converter circuit have been custom-designed and printed on a PCB (Printed Circuit Board) in order to communicate between the on-board processor and the Dynamixel motors

Battery

AcYut uses a 4S Lithium Polymer battery. It provides a voltage of 14.8 volts, ie, the recommended voltage in order to run the motors.

AcYut - meaning 'The Imperishable' in Sanskrit, is the name of the series of humanoids being developed at the Centre for Robotics and Intelligent Systems by a team of undergraduates under the guidance of Dr. B.K. Rout at the Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani, India.
